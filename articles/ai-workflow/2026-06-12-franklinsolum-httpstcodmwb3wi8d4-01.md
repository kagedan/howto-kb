The people getting hired as AI engineers right now are not the ones with the best credentials. They are the ones with the best proof.

That should be the most encouraging sentence you read this year, because proof is the one thing you can manufacture from your laptop, for free, starting tonight.

But first, look at the conveyor belt you were told to ride into tech:

→ get a computer science degree

→ maybe a master's, the field is competitive after all

→ grind LeetCode for a year

→ apply, wait, get filtered by a resume bot

→ repeat until someone gives you permission to build things

That belt was always slow. For AI engineering in 2026, it drops you off at the wrong job entirely. And the machine the internet sells you as the alternative is not much better. Call it what it is, the course hamster wheel:

→ buy the course

→ watch the videos

→ collect the certificate

→ buy the next course

→ wonder why two years passed and you still haven't built anything

If you've been on either machine, here is the part nobody says out loud: the problem was never your discipline or your intelligence. You were strapped into machinery that cannot produce an engineer. Conveyor belts produce graduates. Hamster wheels produce certificates. Only building produces engineers, and the entire game is getting off the machines and onto the bench. This article is the map.

I say this as someone with a folder of course certificates I have never once been asked about, and a handful of small, ugly, working projects that taught me more than all of them combined. The certificates were receipts for time spent. The projects were proof of work. Only one of those two things ever changed what I could do.
