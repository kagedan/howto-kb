# Section 8. Data-Center Hardware: Networks, Optics, Cooling, Power

## 8.1 The ecosystem map: everything between the chip and the megawatt

An AI cluster is not a pile of accelerators but an organism: a hundred thousand chips must talk to each other (networks and optics), must not melt (cooling), and must receive a gigawatt without losses (power). The organism's economics grow non-linearly: doubling a cluster more than doubles the interconnect and the thermal problem — **the share of "everything around the chip" in every construction dollar increases with every generation**. That makes the sector an arsenal multiplier on top of the growth of compute itself.

Three tectonic shifts define the mid-2026 map:

**Shift 1: the network became the compute.** In the era of reasoning models the bottleneck is not one chip's FLOPS but the speed of exchange between chips: training and inference are smeared across thousands of accelerators.
