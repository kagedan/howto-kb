2036: Will You Be Replaced or Upgraded? 🤖🏗️
Most people are panicking about AI, but they’re looking at it all wrong. As someone with a foot in both worlds—Civil Engineering and AI—I’m seeing a massive shift that isn’t making headlines yet. https://t.co/92aJMAC8Ps

The next decade will change everything. 
Here are 10 things you need to know to stay ahead of the curve. 🧵

1️⃣ AI is a Tool, Not a Takeover
AI won’t replace engineers or network admins; people using AI will replace those who don’t. In Civil Engineering, AI handles the math, but humans handle the ethics and safety.

2️⃣Routine is Dead
If your job is 100% repetitive data entry or basic CAD drafting, start pivoting. By 2030, these tasks will be fully automated. The value moves to high-level strategy and system integration. https://t.co/EHURhbYroO

3️⃣The Hybrid Era
The future belongs to the "Hybrid Professional." Knowing how to bridge the gap between physical infrastructure (Civil) and digital infrastructure (Networking) with an AI layer is the ultimate cheat code.

4️⃣Creative Problem Solving &gt; Calculations
AI can calculate structural loads in seconds, but it can’t navigate local politics, land disputes, or the unique cultural context of a construction site in Kaduna. https://t.co/kXmtnH6UYP

5️⃣Networking is Evolving
In 10 years, AI will manage network traffic and security patches autonomously (Intent-Based Networking). Your job? Designing the architecture and governing the AI.

6️⃣The "Human" Premium
As AI-generated content and solutions flood the market, human intuition, empathy, and leadership become luxury skills. Soft skills are now hard requirements.

7️⃣New Roles Will Emerge
Ten years ago, "Prompt Engineer" wasn't a job. By 2035, we’ll have AI Ethicists, Digital Twin Managers, and Automated Infrastructure Overseers. https://t.co/V1YAYyJ2qk
