Creating good prompts is easy when you follow the right framework.

It takes out all of the guesswork and allows you to create highly reliable prompts to automate your tasks.

Here's my step-by-step checklist approach to creating a great prompt. 👇

I've come to this checklist after reading lots of research papers and GitHub repos. And then I've started to compile their best practices for using them to create my own prompts.

Attached below is a (reduced) snippet of one of the prompts I use to edit my writing.

Step 1: Assign a role.

Assigning a role to your AI counterpart will make him adopt the behaviors of the role you give to him.

This one is rather obvious, but often overlooked.

And as you move on further, you will realize that the overarching theme of a good prompt is to control the behavior of the AI, all the while giving him enough room to be creative.

It's really a hard balance to strike, if you ask me.

2. Give it a clear, descriptive, and precisely accurate task.

Next, give a clear, descriptive, and accurate task for it.

If you can provide detailed descriptions in your prompts, the better. Often times, you can even break down bigger tasks into smaller ones.

3. Provide as much context as you can.

Now, provide context. Include everything in your prompt that you think will help it do a better job and produce output that will be useful to you.

In the example below, you will see that I gave it where I plan to publish my writing, what writing style I like, and you can give it a lot more context that you think will help.

So, get creative here.

4. Provide examples.

As some say, show don't tell.

Examples will help the AI visualize exactly what you mean.

So stuff it with examples of the tasks you want it to achieve.

5. Create a list of rules for the AI.

Now, give a list of rules that you want the AI to follow in the final output.
