Construction technology startup @EnlayePro wants to make predictive risk AI a staple of the building industry after closing on a $5 million seed funding round.

The Boston-based startup says that bad data costs the global infrastructure sector a staggering $1.88 trillion in lost value each year, and believes that most of this money can be recouped by digitizing construction site processes.

**AI-Native Risk Management:** Instead of using black-box algorithms, Enlaye has gone for a more transparent approach to AI that relies on graph neural networks and multimodal AI models. Its goal is to enable traceable and explainable AI risk assessments across things like contracts, field reports and schedules and transform them into predictive intelligence that can help prevent disputes before they arise.

- The data bleed: Enlaye is addressing a massive operational void in the construction industry. Studies estimate that around 96% of all data created during construction projects goes unused, with most of it remaining on paper, never transitioning to digital form. Moreover, around 30% of preconstruction data is lost before the average project ends.
- Lack of insight: Contractors, builders, developers and insurers are highly reliant on fragmented legacy systems and manual processes, and these disconnected siloes mean they have no way to capitalize on cross-project insights or learn from previous experiences. This means inefficiencies, duplication, cost overruns, project delays and eroding margins.
- Risk-management lifecycle: Enlaye’s platform consolidates all of this data into a single intelligence layer that spans projects, organizations and time, enabling teams to make smarter decisions, prevent costly claims and disputes and build faster at lower cost.
