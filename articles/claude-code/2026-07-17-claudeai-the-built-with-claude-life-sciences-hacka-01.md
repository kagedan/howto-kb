The Built with Claude: Life Sciences hackathon comes to an end.

Thank you to everyone who spent a week building with Claude Science and Claude Code, and to @gladstoneinst and @cerebral_valley for co-hosting.

Introducing the winners: https://t.co/VTDup1yz9Z

🥇Research track: NCypher by Faith Ogundimu from Dublin, Ireland

A tool for a childhood brain cancer that’s nearly always fatal. It searches the stretches of DNA between genes and narrows thousands of suspect DNA changes down to the handful most worth testing in the lab. https://t.co/yz0Clsvs60

🥈 Research track: Extremolith by Jaymin Patel from Berkeley, CA

A protein design tool that rewrites any enzyme to survive boiling heat, acid, or brine without losing the job it does. The enzymes that digest plastic, for one, work best at temperatures that destroy them. https://t.co/3hWhQBRNaz

🥇 Build track: Lazarus by Dean Sherry from Johannesburg, South Africa

An agent that takes a link to abandoned research code and rebuilds it until it runs, then checks if it still reproduces the numbers from the paper. It fixed a bug sitting in one program for 15 years. https://t.co/j2xAhgY6Js

🥈 Build track: Provinans by Shereen Lee from Toronto, Canada

A tool that stitches decades of cancer records into a single dataset, even when the rules for classifying the disease change. A 2023 revision moved as many as 28% of patients into a different stage overnight. https://t.co/pNGxJgpgxM

🧪 Gladstone Institutes prize: Trialign by Jules Park (Toronto, Canada) and Neil Wang (San Francisco, CA)

A clinical trial matcher for cancer patients  that reads their medical records and finds the studies they qualify for today. https://t.co/gS8aZZIl6T
