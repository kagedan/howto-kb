Claude Design isn't a Figma killer. It's the thing that replaces the step *before* Figma — the one where you stare at a blank canvas and skip visual design entirely because opening Figma feels like homework.

What it actually does: prompt → interactive prototype (not a static mockup). Point it at your codebase, it reads your components and brand tokens. New prototypes match what you've shipped.

The wedge isn't designers. It's every team that didn't have a designer and just shipped whatever shipped first. That's a much bigger market than "replace Figma."

Three prompts worth trying today:

- A settings page for an existing product, matching your brand
- A landing page for a feature you haven't shipped yet
- A dashboard redesign you've been avoiding

Caveat before you build on it: it's research preview, it burns plan quota the same way Claude Code does, and there's no multiplayer. If you need a shared feedback loop, Figma is still your tool.
