Hermes Agent is powerful.

But turning that power into repeatable workflows takes time. You need good planning habits. You need research discipline. You need deep interviews when the request is vague. You need coding handoffs when Hermes should delegate work to Codex, Claude Code, or another executor.

You need evidence boundaries so “prepared”, “running”, “reviewed”, and “verified” do not get mixed together.

That is why I’m building Oh My Hermes.

OMH is a workflow/runtime layer for Hermes Agent.

It gives Hermes a set of strong, practical workflows for the things operators actually do again and again:

- deep interview for unclear goals

- ralplan for serious planning

- ultragoal for ambitious execution paths

- web research for source-backed exploration

- paper-learning for readable paper/PDF explanations

- coding handoff for Codex, Claude Code, Hermes, or other executors

- wiki and long-term memory style workflows

- loop engineering for goals that need repeated research → plan → execute → verify cycles

The idea is simple: Hermes should not just answer.

Hermes should understand what kind of work the user is asking for, route it to the right workflow, prepare the right next step, and keep track of what is actually observed.

Sometimes you talk naturally and OMH routes the request. Sometimes you call a workflow directly. Either way, Hermes gets a stronger operating layer without forcing every user to design their own process from scratch.

OMH is for people who already feel that Hermes is capable, but want it to become more reliable in real work: research, planning, product decisions, coding delegation, paper study, memory, review, and long-running loops.

Hermes is the capable agent.

OMH is the workflow layer that helps Hermes turn capability into repeatable work.
