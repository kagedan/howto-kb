Shortcuts is a weekly guide to the tools, platforms, and setups actually worth your time. Resources you can steal and adapt. To start: the foundation for getting the most out of Claude.

Most people are using about ten percent of what AI can actually do. For a long time, I was one of them.

I started where almost everyone starts: AI as a chat box in a browser tab. You type, it answers. Useful, forgettable, gone by the next session. Most people never move past it. The difference between that and what I run now isn't a smarter model. Everyone has the same models. 

It's everything I built around it.

I’ve loved the wild west learning and building side of the internet since I first got an AOL CD-ROM back in ‘95. The sense of wonder, an endless expanse to explore. To me the internet has always represented “figure it out yourself,” where “it” can be almost anything.

Much of my creative and entrepreneurial path has run alongside learning technical tools. Teaching myself Flash is bizarrely how I broke into nightlife and hospitality: they needed websites, and in that glorious pre-iPhone period Flash animated invites projected ‘cool parties’. Premiere and After Effects led me to editing our early films and the foundations of MATTE's film arm. Every one of those tools started the same way, unfamiliar, learned on the side via internet search.

The tools at our disposal now, the pace of innovation and development, remind me of that early era. The beauty is how open and learnable it all is. **The problem is it's a firehose: daunting, fast-moving, and I imagine paralyzing.** For that reason I'm starting a weekly column synthesizing what I'm seeing, what I'm using, what's productive and what's noise. I want to share resources, tools, actual setups you can steal, it will be called ***‘Shortcut*s’**

**Part 01 is sharing the bedrock of my operating setup.** What I've spent the past 9 months building is a foundation
