Notify me when the tests finish
now becomes a phone push. 
Claude Code added that in 2.1.110 on April 15, 2026.

The obvious read is convenience. The more interesting read is workflow.

Coding agents used to assume you'd sit there and watch. A phone push changes that. Now the software assumes the task can keep running, and the human only needs to show up at the boundary.

That matters because the session still lives on your machine. Your files, your tools, your MCP setup, your project state. The phone isn't replacing the laptop. It's becoming a control surface for a local session that keeps working without your eyes on it.

Once that happens, the bottleneck stops looking like model speed and starts looking like human approval latency. The agent can keep going. The delay is often the moment where it needs a person to check, approve, or decide.

So the real shift is not "Claude got mobile." It's that coding agents are starting to act less like assistants you watch and more like systems that page you when your judgment is needed.
