Shopify just dropped their AI Toolkit today. It integrates with Claude Code, Codex, Cursor, VS Code. This is genuinely cool and I'm excited about it.

But I need to say this clearly: please do not just click "SEO optimize" and expect good outcomes.

Some stores can get away with that. If you sell 15 SKUs with incredibly literal product names and clean collection architecture, sure, auto-generated meta titles and descriptions might be fine (though you still likely need to go after easier longtail variations first then scale into general broad keywords).

That is not most stores.

90% of the Shopify brands I work with have major SEO problems because they've published dozens of pages that compete with each other for the same keywords. Collections overlapping with blog posts. Product pages cannibalizing collection pages. Landing pages nobody remembers creating that are quietly stealing authority from the pages that actually matter.

When you layer AI-generated SEO copy on top of that mess, you're not optimizing. You're reinforcing the problem. You're telling Google "yes, I definitely want all six of these pages to rank for the same term" and Google responds by ranking none of them.

Fixing keyword cannibalization can take a long time. Sometimes months. It's one of the most tedious, high-stakes SEO problems in ecommerce because every URL change, every redirect, every consolidation carries risk and needs direct approval from business owners/leaders. It costs a lot of money to unwind.

The AI toolkit is a powerful accelerator. But an accelerator on a broken foundation just gets you to the wrong destination faster.

Audit your pages first. Strategize. Then optimize.
