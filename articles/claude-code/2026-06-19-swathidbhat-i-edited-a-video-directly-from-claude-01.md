I edited a video directly from Claude Code Opus 4.8 and nearly cried with joy. A simple video to support a post I wrote on building games with different tools. This is what Claude did for me:

Creation
- ran a simple python file with a tic-tac-toe game that I'd built and recorded a video of it playing
- created a sound clip by itself

Editing/ Chores
- stitch together a few other pre-recorded video clips 
- add titles 
- trim the video: I didn't even have to explicitly give claude timestamps. it visually reasoned by looking at frames and suggested trims itself 
- download a sound clip that I created on Suno

Analysis/ Ideation 
- I asked it to suggest ideas for changes after the first iteration and gave some good options, given that I was optimizing for speed

Some limitations
Claude was good at "seeing" but couldn't hear and explicitly asked for my ears to test. Sound was the only bit where I had to get quite involved. Claude refused to source & download 3rd party music files even if license-free. I decided to generate a soundclip on Suno. It agreed to download my clip after I explicitly stated that I had generated it. Sensible license gates! 

It's not a super complex video or a workflow (for now). But I'm still very excited by this because I've done the manual workflows a couple of times, and can't wait to hand it over!
