*The Fireworks AI CEO processes 40 trillion tokens a day and thinks the capex-bubble crowd has the arithmetic backwards. This is her on Harry Stebbings' 20VC talking about how her real push is about who gets to own intelligence.*

---

**The short version, if you don't have the hour:**

- Fireworks processes more than 40 trillion tokens a day, most of them from customized models rather than off-the-shelf ones, and Qiao expects that volume to grow 20 to 100x by the end of next year on what she calls the early stage of an S-curve.
- She sees token costs falling roughly 10x over three years, and that drop pulling usage up about 100x. Costs haven't dropped yet because of a supply-chain shortage in energy, chips, and manufacturing rather than any ceiling on the technology.
- Gross margins of 30 to 40 percent, against the 80 percent a SaaS business earns, are a hypergrowth choice rather than the new normal. Fireworks won't move into the application layer, and moving down into data centers is a question of timing.
- Revenue is near $800 million dollars of ARR and she expects it to at least double by year end, with 200 people.
- Frontier labs like OpenAI and Anthropic are building a power line to distribute intelligence, foundational and valuable, but in her read they will not replace the specialized intelligence every company builds on top. She does not want a world where one company owns intelligence.
- Open and closed models have both crossed a quality threshold. Open weights give control, are far easier to tune, and a small amount of a company's unique data can hill-climb an open model past a general-purpose one on that company's own task.
- Last year was the year of coding, and this year is the year of co-work, a far more diversified set of jobs across legal, finance, support, recruiting, sales, and healthcare.
- Hardware used to depreciate over six years. Now one vendor ships three chip generations a year and models turn over
