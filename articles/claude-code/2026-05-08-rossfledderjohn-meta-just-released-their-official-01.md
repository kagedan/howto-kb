Meta just released their official Ads MCP server and CLI.

Both are live in open beta right now - available to all eligible advertisers globally. No developer credentials, API setup, or coding required.

This is not read-only access.

You get full read and write - create campaigns, edit ad sets, update budgets, run diagnostics.

An LLM can now operate inside your Meta Ads account directly.

4 things it can do right now:

1. Comprehensive reporting - pull detailed reports and surface trends
2. Campaign management - create and edit without clicking through Ads Manager
3. Catalog management - build catalogs and troubleshoot feed issues
4. Signal diagnostics - check signal health across your setup

They also dropped a CLI.

The Ads CLI runs Meta Ads commands from your terminal - compatible with AI agents in Claude Code, Codex, or any shell-based automation workflow.

Two tools. One for chat. One for agents.

The practical shift is real.

Manual Meta Ads reporting eats 2-4 hours a week. Export, clean, visualize, analyze, communicate.

With this, you generate a full campaign report with executive summary and action items in under 60 seconds.

Why is Meta doing this?

They want more advertisers on their platform. Opening to third-party AI tools keeps you in the Meta ecosystem instead of moving to cross-channel alternatives.

It is an opening up and a lock-in move at the same time.

The honest caveat:

Meta's algorithm - Advantage+ and their auto-optimization layers - already handles micro-adjustments.

The real power is not flipping bids every 15 minutes. It is getting insight Meta does not surface well - cross-account rollups, creative fatigue, why winning ads win.

The roles of data analyst, media buyer, and creative strategist have been quietly merging.

Marketers are collapsing workflows that used to live in 5 tabs, 3 spreadsheets, and a dashboard into a single conversation with an LLM.

Meta's MCP is the infrastructure layer that makes that collapse possible.
