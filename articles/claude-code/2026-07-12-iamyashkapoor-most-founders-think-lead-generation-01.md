Most founders think lead generation is a volume problem.

It's not.

It's a research problem.

I watched a Claude Code + Clay workflow that fixes this completely.

Here are the 10 steps, broken down simply: 🧵

Step 1: Define your ICP in plain English.

Not "SaaS founders."

Something like this:

"B2B SaaS, 10 to 50 employees, raised a Series A, uses HubSpot, posted a sales hire in the last 30 days."

The sharper this is, the better everything after it works.

Step 2: Claude Code turns that description into a real search.

You are not filling out filters.

You are describing what you want in normal words.

Claude figures out how to find it.

Step 3: Clay handles the enrichment.

Clay pulls verified emails, tech stack, headcount, and hiring signals from dozens of sources in one pass.

Clay is not doing the thinking here.

It is just feeding clean data to Claude.

Step 4: Claude Code scores each lead.

Not with a basic point system in a spreadsheet.

With actual reasoning.

"This company hired 3 SDRs last month and just switched CRMs. That is a hot lead."

Claude can explain why a lead is good, not just rank it.

Step 5: Personalized first lines, written automatically.

Not "I saw your company does great work."

More like:

"Saw you just moved from Salesforce to HubSpot. Curious if outbound is next on the list."

Specific. Fast. For every single lead.

Step 6: Everything routes straight to your CRM.

No CSV export.
No manual upload.
No copy and paste.

The lead lands in HubSpot or Salesforce already scored and ready to work.

Step 7: Replies get classified automatically.

Interested. Not now. Unsubscribe. Out of office. Referral.

This runs at over 94 percent accuracy without any extra training.

No lead falls through because someone forgot to check an inbox.
