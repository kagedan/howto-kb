For years, programmatic SEO was the best trick in the game. 

One template, one dataset, thousands of pages hitting every long-tail keyword. 

You could rank a whole category while your competitors published one blog a week.

It was the strategy behind the success of known names like Pieter Levels and Danny Postma... *but it stopped working for most people.*

## What actually killed it

Google's March 2024 core update, which folded the helpful content system into core ranking and added a new spam policy: scaled content abuse. 

In Google's own words, "producing content at scale for the purpose of ranking" now works against you. Google says the change cut low-quality, unoriginal results by about 45%.

![](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HMaGXGCXEAA_NIb.jpg)

Programmatic SEO was volume with no substance.

But the idea behind it was never wrong. You still want coverage at reasonable scale. You just cannot fake the value anymore.

## The Importance of E-E-A-T

Besides not starving :)   

Google grades content on four things: Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trustworthiness. That is E-E-A-T.

> The first "E", Experience, is the newest and the one that matters most right now. It asks a blunt question: has the writer actually done the thing? Real results, first-hand knowledge, lived opinions.

That is the new angle.  *Smaller scale based on high-intent.*
