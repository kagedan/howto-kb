One research task adds 10% to your Claude Code context window. Or 0.5%. The difference is whether you used a sub-agent.

Carl Vellotti showed the receipts live. Same research query. Without a sub-agent, context jumped from 16% to 25%. With a sub-agent, 16% to 16.5%. The sub-agent used 10 tool calls and 30,000 tokens, all outside the main session. Your working memory never felt it.

This is why most people hit the wall after 45 minutes. They're loading MCPs that eat context just by existing. They're running research inline instead of delegating. They're watching the green bar turn red before they've even started working.

The hierarchy almost everyone gets backwards: CLIs cost zero context. APIs cost medium. MCPs cost the most because they're always loaded. Karpathy confirmed the same thing independently.

The people running Claude Code 8+ hours a day figured this out months ago. They stopped treating it like a chat window. They built operating systems. Persistent markdown files that compound across sessions. People dossiers that make every stakeholder message specific instead of generic. Skills that screenshot their own output and self-correct before you ever see it.

The compounding loop is the whole game. You update a people file after a meeting. Next time you draft a message to that person, Claude already knows their communication style, their concerns from last week, their pet peeves. The output goes from "sounds like AI" to "sounds like someone who was in the room."

Third episode with Carl. First two crossed a million views. This one is the operating system layer.
