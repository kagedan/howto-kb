The real shift in AI coding isn't about which tool wins — it's about what happens when the tools become invisible infrastructure.

@David_TornAI is right. The debate over Claude Code vs Cursor vs Codex misses the point. They're all solving different problems at different stages of the workflow.

But here's what nobody's saying out loud:

In six months, the tool you choose won't matter. Because they'll all be good enough. The differentiation will collapse. The magic will be commoditized.

What'll matter then? The same thing that's always mattered: taste, speed, and distribution.

— Taste: knowing what to build and what to cut
— Speed: shipping before the market moves
— Distribution: getting it in front of people who'll pay

AI tools make the building part easy. They don't make the judgment calls easy. They don't make the marketing easy.

I can build a feature in Prospel in an afternoon now. The hard part? Deciding if it's the right feature. And then convincing someone it's worth their money.

The tool is the entry ticket. Your instincts are the competitive advantage.
